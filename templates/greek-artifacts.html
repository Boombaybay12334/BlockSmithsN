Apoxyomenos (Youth Scrapings Himself)

Details: The statue is made of bronze and stands approximately 65 cm (25.6 inches) tall. It is in excellent condition, considering its age, with intricate details and a beautiful patina. The statue is mounted on a custom-made marble base.
Origin: Greece, circa 1st century BCE
History: This exquisite bronze statue is a rare example of a Greek sculpture from the Hellenistic period. It depicts a youthful athlete scraping himself with a strigil, a tool used to remove oil and dirt from the skin after exercise. The statue is a testament to the Greek obsession with physical beauty and athleticism.
Place: Found in a shipwreck off the coast of Greece, near the island of Antikythera Details: The statue is made of bronze and stands approximately 65 cm (25.6 inches) tall.
