Navajo Rug

Details: Material: Wool Dimensions: Varies, but commonly around 3 feet by 5 feet Condition: Excellent, with vibrant colors and no visible damage Design: Handwoven, featuring traditional patterns, colors, and motifs unique to the Navajo culture.
History: Navajo rugs are woven textiles created by the Navajo people, with a rich history that dates back to the late 19th century. They are renowned for their intricate designs and high-quality craftsmanship. Originally, these rugs were made for practical uses, such as blankets and floor coverings, but they have since become highly sought after as art pieces. The designs often incorporate geometric patterns and symbols that carry cultural significance, reflecting the natural world and the Navajo way of life.
Place: This artifact originates from the Navajo Nation, which spans parts of Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah.
Price: The price for a Navajo rug can range from $200 to several thousand dollars, depending on the size, age, and complexity of the design

Sioux Beaded Pipe Bag

Details: Material: Leather, glass beads, and sinew Dimensions: Approximately 24 inches in length Condition: Good, with some wear typical of age, but overall intact Design: Features vibrant beadwork with traditional patterns, often including motifs such as animals, flowers, or geometric shapes.
History: The Sioux beaded pipe bag is a traditional item used by the Sioux tribes of the Great Plains, particularly during ceremonial occasions. Pipe bags are often used to carry ceremonial pipes, which are important in spiritual and social rituals among Native American cultures. The intricate beadwork on these bags often represents the identity and status of the owner, as well as their connection to the spiritual world.
Place: This artifact originates from the Sioux Nation, which primarily resides in the northern Great Plains region of the United States, including North Dakota, South Dakota, Nebraska, and Montana.
Price: The price for a Sioux beaded pipe bag can range from $300 to $2,000, depending on the intricacy of the beadwork, age, and provenance of the item.

Navajo Silver and Turquoise Bracelet

Details: Material: Sterling silver and turquoise Dimensions: Typically around 6 to 8 inches in circumference, adjustable Condition: Excellent, with no visible damage (condition may vary based on the specific piece) Design: Features intricate silverwork, often with stamped designs and inlaid turquoise stones.
History: Navajo jewelry-making has a rich history that dates back to the late 19th century, influenced by both traditional Native American techniques and the introduction of silver by European settlers. The use of turquoise is significant in Navajo culture, symbolizing protection, healing, and good fortune. The craftsmanship of Navajo silversmiths is renowned for its intricate designs and high-quality materials. Bracelets like this one are often worn during ceremonies and special occasions, as well as being popular among collectors and jewelry enthusiasts. Place: This artifact originates from the Navajo Nation, which spans parts of Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah.
Price: The price for a Navajo silver and turquoise bracelet can range from $100 to $1,500, depending on the intricacy of the design, the quality of the materials, and the reputation of the artist.
